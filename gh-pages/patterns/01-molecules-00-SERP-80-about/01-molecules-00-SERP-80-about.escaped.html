<div class="well"> <h3>About</h3> <p>This search result mock-up is a starting point for teams building enterprise search front ends, showing best practices. Over time I hope to store it in a Pattern Lab repository. The mock-up will then be part of a larger project with the working title <strong>«<a href="https://github.com/bjornte/UX-patterns-for-findability/">UX patterns for findability</a>»</strong>.</p> <p>Findability is the key success factor of any search, and is the most important motivation for starting this project. To improve findability, the mock-up has an emphasis on:</p> <ul> <li><span class="comment" data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="top" title="Disambiguation: To identify the meaning of a word, when the word has multiple meanings.">Disambiguation</span> through 1) Result groups and 2) Autocomplete topics</li> <li>User influenced relevancy &amp; findability</li> <li>User added results &amp; sources</li> <li>Editorial maintenance</li> <li>Findability reporting</li> </ul> <p>For more on these topics, see the <a href="https://github.com/bjornte/UX-patterns-for-findability/#focus-areas">introduction on GitHub</a>. Also see the <span class="comment" data-toggle="tooltip" data-placement="top" title="Comments look like this.">inline comments</span> for more details. Reload to show.</span>)</p> <p>Hopefully the rationale will be fleshed out in an accompanying presentation.</p> <p>Content is in part sampled from <a href="http://www.nrk.no">NRK</a> and <a href="http://www.theguardian.com">The Guardian</a>.
